Sisig

Description

Sisig (/ˈsiːsɪɡ/ Tagalog pronunciation: ['sisig]) is a Filipino dish made from pork jowl and ears (maskara), pork belly, and chicken liver, which is usually seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers.

Ingredients

  • Pork (usually pork belly or pork cheeks), diced or minced
  • Onion, finely chopped
  • Garlic, minced
  • Chili peppers (optional), chopped
  • Calamansi or lime juice
  • Soy sauce
  • Mayonnaise
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Green onions (scallions), chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Preparation: Boil the pork belly and pork liver until cooked. Once done, chop them into small pieces.
  2. Sautéing: In a pan, heat some oil over medium heat. Sauté the minced garlic and onion until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Adding Meat: Add the chopped pork belly and liver to the pan. Stir-fry for a few minutes until slightly browned and crispy.
  4. Seasoning: Pour in the soy sauce and calamansi juice. Mix well. Add the minced chili peppers. Stir and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Mayonnaise: Add the mayonnaise to the pan and mix thoroughly with the meat. Let it cook for another minute or two, allowing the flavors to blend together.
  6. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the sisig and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper according to your preference.
  7. Serving: Transfer the sisig to a serving plate. Garnish with chopped green onions and fried garlic for extra flavor and texture. Optionally, you can top it with a fried egg for a richer experience.
  8. Enjoy: Serve your homemade sisig hot and enjoy it with steamed rice or as a pulutan (appetizer) with your favorite drink.
